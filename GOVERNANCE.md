# Governing BRACE

BRACE is an open, vendor-neutral technical control framework for autonomous AI agents, released under CC BY 4.0. It is run in the open and built to be shared. This document explains how decisions get made and how to take on a bigger role.

## Principles

- **Open by default.** Discussion, decisions, and the roadmap live in public issues and pull requests.
- **Evidence-grounded.** Controls are justified by real incidents and in-production practice, not opinion. New claims should point to something concrete.
- **Vendor-neutral.** We describe what products do; we don't endorse. No vendor controls the framework.
- **Composes, doesn't compete.** BRACE is the control layer that sits alongside OWASP, NIST, and MITRE — not a replacement.

## Roles

- **Contributors** — anyone who opens an issue, comments, or sends a pull request. No commitment required.
- **Maintainers** — review and merge changes, triage issues, and steer the roadmap. Maintainers are stewards, not owners; they are listed in the Maintainers section below.

## How decisions get made

- Most changes: open an issue, discuss, then a pull request. Lazy consensus — with no objections in a reasonable window, a maintainer merges.
- Changes to a control (C1–C9) or observability requirement (T1–T3): open an issue first (see `CONTRIBUTING.md`). These are the core of the framework, so changes are deliberate.
- Unresolved disagreements: maintainers decide in the open, with the reasoning recorded on the issue.

## Becoming a maintainer

Sustained, high-quality contributions — solid RFCs, accurate vendor mappings, well-sourced incidents, careful reviews — lead to an invitation from the current maintainers.

**BRACE is early and actively looking for co-maintainers.** If you build or secure autonomous agents and want a real say in this framework, start with a few contributions and say you're interested. Shared ownership is the goal, not a formality.
